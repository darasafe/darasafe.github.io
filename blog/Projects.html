Projects

I have engaged in several projects between my schooling and work experience. My most recent non-work projects were an independent study, a gearbox assembly design, and developing an app called Helix.

Independent Study

Over a semester, ​I explored hydrogen as a potential alternative fuel source in industrial steel reheat furnaces and evaluated the feasibility of specific hydrogen production pathways. ​My interest in the topic is linked with my desire to explore clean energy sources to reduce carbon emissions across energy-intensive industries, such as the steel industry.

Helix App

I co-founded an app called Helix, a table reservation service for busy venues near college campuses. We offer a unique way for patrons to reserve tables and booths ahead of time. I worked on the app and web page's user interface and user experience, as well as led the project's marketing strategy. I developed the business plan for investors and have signed 10+ prospective venues before our launch date.

Moped Gearbox Assembly Design

I was part of a team of four that designed a gearpod assembly for a moped. We performed detailed stress and fatigue analysis based on the gearbox configuration, determined appropriate material selection for each component, and created a fully dimensioned drawing of all gear shafts. The resulting gearbox was deisgned to be cost effective, durable, and lightweight.

A Comparative Analysis of Hydrogen and Natural Gas as Reducing Agents in Steelmaking

I led a team of three undergraduates on a research project that compares natural gas and hydrogen as a reducing agent in the primary steelmaking route in terms of carbon intensity, production efficiency, and cost of implementation.

Renewable Energy System Modeling

I created a System Advisor Model to produce 1000MWh per year in Inglewood, California using solar panels with single-axis tracking, while minimizing system complexity and Levelized Cost of Electricity (LCOE).

F.H. King Bike Compost Cart

I worked in a team of four undergraduates to design, build, and test a bike compost trailer than can safely support up to four hundred pounds of compost for the F.H. King student organization.
