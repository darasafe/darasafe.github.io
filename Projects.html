Projects

I have engaged in several projects between my schooling and work experience. My latest non-work projects include Senior Design, research at HERD, and an independent study on hydrogen application.

Senior Design

I am team leader of a senior design project focused on developing an innovative device to monitor insect populations and diversity using computer vision technology in terrestrial ecosystems. The project aims to design and build an automated insect camera light trap that can capture high-quality images of insects to improve the efficiency of insect monitoring in a cost-effective way.

Hydrogen and Electrochemical Research for Decarbonization (HERD) Lab

At the HERD lab, my research focuses on the study of perovskite materials for use in air electrodes within solid oxide electrolysis and metal-air battery cells. After establishing the key criteria for optimized performance, my next step is to examine various perovskite manufacturing methods for lab-scale production. In addition to this, I collaborate closely with the principal investigator, Professor Mastropasqua, on the development of innovative electrochemical cell technologies.

Independent Study - Hydrogen Integration in Industrial Furnaces

I conducted research on the use of hydrogen as a potential alternative fuel for industrial steel reheat furnaces and assessed the viability of various hydrogen production methods. My enthusiasm for this subject stems from my aspiration to investigate clean energy sources that can minimize carbon emissions in energy-intensive sectors, such as the steel industry.

Helix App

I played a key role in founding Helix, a table reservation service for popular venues near college campuses. My responsibilities included designing the app and web interface, leading the marketing strategy, developing the business plan, and securing commitments from over 10 potential venues prior to our launch.

Moped Gearbox Assembly Design

I worked with a team of four to design a gearpod assembly for a moped. We performed detailed stress and fatigue analysis based on the gearbox configuration, determined appropriate material selection for each component, and created a fully dimensioned drawing of all gear shafts. The resulting gearbox was deisgned to be cost effective, durable, and lightweight.

A Comparative Analysis of Hydrogen and Natural Gas as Reducing Agents in Steelmaking

I supervised a research project with a team of three undergraduate students that analyzed the effectiveness of natural gas and hydrogen as reducing agents in the primary steelmaking process, considering factors such as carbon emissions, production efficiency, and implementation costs.

Solar Renewable Energy System Model

I designed a System Advisor Model (SAM) aimed at generating 1000MWh annually in Inglewood, California through the use of single-axis tracking solar panels. My main focus was to reduce system complexity while keeping the Levelized Cost of Electricity (LCOE) at a minimum.
